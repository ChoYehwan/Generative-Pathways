# Research Questions

## Main Research Question

How do Diffusion Models and Flow Matching differ in their probability paths from a prior distribution to a data distribution, and how does this difference affect sampling efficiency and sample trajectories?

## Sub Questions

1. How can generative models be interpreted as transformations from a simple prior distribution to a complex data distribution?

2. What is the mathematical connection between Neural ODEs, Continuous Normalizing Flows, and Flow Matching?

3. How do Diffusion Models generate samples through iterative denoising?

4. How does Flow Matching learn a velocity field along a probability path?

5. How do sample trajectories differ between Diffusion Models and Flow Matching?

6. How does generation quality change as the number of function evaluations, NFE, decreases?

## Research Position

This project does not aim to prove that Flow Matching is always superior to Diffusion Models. Instead, it aims to understand both methods as probability-path-based generative models and analyze how their path design affects sampling behavior.
